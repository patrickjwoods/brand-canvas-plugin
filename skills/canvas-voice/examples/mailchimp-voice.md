# Mailchimp — Section 4: Voice & Expression

_Completed example for reference. Primary example used throughout the book._

---

## Values

1. **Humility** — We're not the smartest people in the room and we don't pretend to be. Our users are experts in their businesses; we're experts in marketing tools. We learn from them.
2. **Creativity** — We believe making things is meaningful. We bring genuine aesthetic investment to everything we build, and we encourage our users to bring theirs.
3. **Independence** — We're independently owned. Our users are independent business owners. We're aligned at the level of fundamental conviction: being able to do things your own way matters.
4. **Community** — Small businesses are the fabric of local economies. We feel a genuine responsibility to the community of entrepreneurs we serve.
5. **Generosity** — The free tier isn't a marketing tactic — it's a value. We give before we ask.
6. **Weirdness** — Freddie von Chimpenheimer IV is a mascot for a reason. We're not a corporate software company. We embrace the quirky, the unexpected, the human.
7. **Grit** — Small business is hard. We know that. We respect the people who do it and we build tools that match their resilience.

---

## Personality

**Adjectives:** Quirky, positive, friendly, passionate, empathetic, inspirational

**Worldview:** Mailchimp believes that small businesses deserve the same marketing tools as big ones — without having to act like a big one. The company has a genuine affection for the idiosyncrasy of small business: the handmade product, the local shop, the side hustle that became something real. It speaks to customers like a knowledgeable friend who genuinely wants you to succeed, not like a consultant billing by the hour.

Mailchimp would say: "The best marketing doesn't feel like marketing — it feels like hearing from someone you like."

**Spectrums:**
- Formal ↔ **Casual** (strongly casual — conversational, uses contractions, talks to the user like a friend)
- Serious ↔ **Playful** (leans playful — comfortable with humor, uses Freddie as a character, takes the work seriously but doesn't take itself too seriously)
- **Established** ↔ Challenger (Mailchimp has earned category authority; it doesn't speak like an upstart)

## Key Messages

**1. Marketing for the rest of us**
Email marketing used to require a team and a budget. Mailchimp changed that. The platform is built so that a one-person business can do what Fortune 500 marketing departments do — and look just as good doing it.

Proof points:
- 12 million customers worldwide, most of them small businesses
- Free tier lets anyone start for $0
- Drag-and-drop editor requires zero design or coding knowledge

Type: Inarguably true

---

**2. Be yourself, on a bigger stage**
You built your business with personality, conviction, and a point of view. Your marketing should reflect that — not sand it down into something corporate and impersonal. Mailchimp helps you stay authentic as you grow.

Proof points:
- Brand customization in every template
- Voice and tone guide that celebrates personality
- Freddie — the mascot — is proof that we mean it about weirdness

Type: Aspirational (and increasingly true)

---

**3. Your audience is waiting for you**
Most small business owners underestimate how much their existing customers *want* to hear from them. Email isn't interruption — it's invitation. Mailchimp gives you the tools to reach the people who already like you and turn them into the people who can't imagine not having you around.

Proof points:
- Average email open rates for small businesses are 3–5x higher than social media reach
- Welcome email automation converts at 50%+ for most businesses
- Customer retention is 5x cheaper than acquisition

Type: Inarguably true (with proof)

---

**4. Grow without losing yourself**
The biggest fear for small business owners isn't growth — it's what growth costs. That you'll become something impersonal, corporate, automated. Mailchimp's thesis is that you can grow and stay human. You can reach more people without becoming a different kind of company.

Proof points:
- Independence from external investors (retained authentic voice)
- Continuous investment in small-business-specific features and education
- Customer stories that celebrate character, not just scale

Type: Aspirational
